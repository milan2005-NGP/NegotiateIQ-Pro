🎙️ Negotiation Scripts
Opening Move
"Thank you so much for the offer. I'm genuinely excited about this role and the team. I did want to discuss the compensation — based on my research and experience, I was expecting something in the range of [Target]. Is there flexibility there?"
💡 Always anchor with gratitude first. Never name a number below your target.
Counter Offer
"I appreciate you working with me on this. I'm still a bit below where I need to be. Given my [specific skill/achievement], would you be able to get closer to [counter number]?"
💡 Always give a reason. Never counter without justification.
If They Say No
"I understand there may be constraints. Could we explore other parts of the package — a signing bonus, extra PTO, or an earlier performance review at 6 months?"
💡 Never accept the first "no". Always negotiate the full package.
Closing
"This sounds like a great fit, and I'm excited to contribute. I'll take a day to review everything carefully and get back to you tomorrow — does that work?"
💡 Never accept on the spot. Always take 24 hours minimum.

✅ Negotiation Prep Checklist
Research market salary ranges for your exact role + location
Use AmbitionBox, Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary
Know your BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement)
What's your walkaway point? Do you have competing offers?
List 3 specific achievements with measurable impact
Numbers talk: "increased sales by 40%", "reduced bugs by 60%"
Decide your target, realistic, and minimum salary
Target = 20% above minimum. Ask for target, accept realistic.
Practice your opening script out loud 5 times
Sounds awkward in your head? It won't once you say it aloud.
Prepare for "What's your current salary?" question
You can decline to share in most states/countries. Redirect to expectations.
Know the full package: bonus, equity, PTO, remote, health
Salary is just one lever. Total comp matters.
Never accept on the spot — always ask for 24 hours
